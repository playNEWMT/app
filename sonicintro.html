Information about Sonic Introduction

Sonic Introduction is an instrument broken up into two sections, Tap and Loop. You can connect both to the same Sensor or each to different Sensors.

For either section, the volume can be adjusted with the "Vol" slider, it looks like an ear!

Tap

This section activates a sound when you pass a certain threshold.

To change which Sensor controls Tap, choose a sensor from the radio buttons directly under the title.

Once you've connected a Sensor the dark pink bar should move around in accordance to your Sensor.

Choose a sound from the top dropdown menu. It starts with the sound "access granted"

You can hear the sound you've selected using the "Test Sound" button.

To change the threshold, move the big orange arrow. When the dark pink bar moves past the orange arrow, the sound will play.

In Accelerometer mode, the dark pink indicator will stay in the middle portion of it's slider.

In Gyroscope mode, the indicator will travel beyond the middle section of the slider.

Gyroscope mode is useful for creating drums, it uses a flicking motion, whereas Accelerometer mode uses a tilting motion.

To change modes on your Sensor, hit the "change mode" button on your Sensor.

Loop

This section repeats a sound when you move your Sensor off axis.

The indicator slider will move with your sensor and the more you move off axis, the louder

You can change the Senstivity slider to alter how much tilt you need to make the Sensor activate the sound.

The Loop section works best when using the Sensor's Accelerometer mode.

Extended Techniques

You can open multiple tabs of Sonic Introduction to achieve advanced behaviors.

Three tabs, of either Tap or Loop, can be opened and connected to the same Sensor but on different axis in order to active different sounds when moving the Sensor along each axis.

You can connected Tap and Loop to the same sensor.
