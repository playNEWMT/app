    Try connecting to a sensor!

    • Turn your sensor on and hit the connect button to pair your sensor.
    • The Sensor Sidebar will open, showing you information about your sensor
    • In the Sensor Sidebar you can change the sensor's axis.
    • Pressing the center button on your sensor will toggle the mode between Accelerometer and Gyroscope
    • Accelerometer is for tilting and Gyroscope is for flicking
    • Close the Sensor Sidebar by clicking the sensor button.
    • The disconnect button (also in the Sensor Sidebar) allows your to unpair a sensor.
    • (Note: You will need to connect on each instrument page)

    Our Instruments

    Sonic Introduction

    Sonic Introduction guides students into the world of digital music creation by introducing two instruments, tap and loop, that reflect two different approaches to the creation of sound.

    Rainstick

    Rainstick uses a sensor to control audio playback. Just as in an acoustic rainstick where grains flow with tilt, the digital version plays short audio "grains" in response to tilting a sensor.

    Wearable Jazz

    Wearable Jazz is a virtual ensemble made up of different instruments or effects that can be controlled by up to three sensors. Sensors can control Drums and Chords, Melody, and Effects.

    Soundboard

    Soundboard is designed for the Sports Orchestra Lesson of our curriculum. It allows teachers to play five different baseball related sounds in order to be the "conductor" or the sports orchestra.

    Indepth Instrument Overview

    These instruments can be used stand-alone, or you can open multiple tabs of our instruments and connect sensors to instruments across tabs to achieve more complex behavior. For example, "Tap", one half of Sonic Introduction, only works on one axis of the sensor at a time. However, if there are two instances of Tap open in different tabs, connected to the same sensor on different axis, you can play a two different sounds by moving a sensor along the two axis. The possibilties open up from there. Rainstick and Loop can be combined to create ambient soundscapes, or three different instances of rainstick can form a tripple axis sound generating experience.
    Below are some detail descriptions of our instruments:

    Sonic Introduction

    Sonic Introduction is two intruments in one. Each explore different paradigms in music making: the one-shot, and the loop. A one-shot is meant to only play once at a specified time, whereas a loop plays continously with the choice to fade it in or out depending on the context.
    1. Tap: Play a sound once
    Use your sensor to initiate the playback of a sound. You can choose which sound to play, as well as the threshold of tilt or movement required to activate it
    2. Loop: Play a sound forever
    Looping is an important technique in digital music, since it allows sounds of any length to play forever. With this instrument, the sensor controls the loudness of the sound, but the loop is always playing behind the scenes, even if you don’t hear it.

    Rainstick

    This digital Rainstick closely emulates an acoustic one; however, the user is able to choose what the grains sound like from a bank of sounds. Tilt the sensor to activate the Rainstick. The steeper the tilt, the faster these sounds play. Users can customize the maximum rate at which the grains play, select the number of audio grains, and change their size and pitch. You can even introduce different kinds of randomization to more closely simulate the physical attributes of a real rainstick.

    Wearable Jazz

    Wearable Jazz contains three parts that students can control with one, two, or three sensors. Each of these parts are different roles in a musical ensemble:
    - The Drum & Chord Sensor makes the sound of a bass and snare drum and sets the mood by changing musical chords.
    - The Melody Sensor plays notes that match the chord. A probability control affects how often notes will be played.
    - The Sound Effects Sensor lets you change how our music sounds, adding rhythmic effects to the music and allowing selection of filtering, echo, or glitch effects that change the sound of the whole ensemble.
