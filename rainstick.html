Information about Rainstick

Rainstick allows you to control a virtual rainstick with your Sensor. Choose an axis in the Sensor Sidebar and tilt it to play a sound as grains in a rainstick.

Rainstick works best when your Sensor is in Accelerometer mode.

Top Controls

Choose which Sensor is connected using "connected to:" under the title.

Choose a sound using the sound dropdown, and test your sound using the "Test Sound" button

"Vol" makes Rainstick quieter or louder.

"Speed" changes how fast the individual grains in the rainstick fall. A slow trickle vs a fast stream.

The purple indicator in the middle shows how the Rainstick is reacting to your Sensor movement.

Grain Controls

"number" adds more grains to the Rainstick as you turn it up

"size" changes how much of a chunk each grain is out of the original sound your chose.

"pitch" changes how high or low pitch your sound is.

"randomness" adds a bit of organic chaos as you turn it up.

Extended Techniques

Turn up the "randomness" to make Rainstick sound more like an acoustic Rainstick.

Three tabs can be opened and connected to the same Sensor but on different axis in order to active different sounds when moving the Sensor along each axis.
